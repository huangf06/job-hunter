## Likely Questions & Prepared Answers

### Q1: "Tell me about yourself" / "Walk me through your background"
**Strategy:** 90 seconds max. Chronological but themed.

"I started in data analysis at Ele.me — one of China's largest food delivery platforms — where I worked with massive datasets and SQL-heavy workloads. Then I moved into quantitative trading at Baiquan Investment, building real-time data pipelines for market data. At GLP Technology, I was the first technical hire for data engineering. ### Q2: "Why Aon?"
**Strategy:** Show you've done research. Reference the AAU program and Broker Copilot.

"Three reasons. First, the migration project — consolidating Oracle, Synapse, and AWS Databricks onto Azure Databricks is a once-in-a-career architecture challenge. I want to be the person who designs that target state. Second, I read about the Aon Broker Copilot and the billion-dollar digital transformation commitment — the data platform I'd build directly feeds those AI products. That's not just plumbing; it's enabling the company's strategic direction. Third, Aon's scale — $17 billion, 60,000 people, 120 countries — means the data problems are genuinely complex. And I know the Reinsurance team already uses Delta Sharing across decentralized lakehouses, so I'd be joining a data-mature organization, not starting from scratch."

### Q3: "Why Lead? You don't have 7+ years of pure DE"
**Strategy:** Reframe quality over quantity.

"At GLP, I was the first data person. I didn't inherit processes — I created them. I selected the tech stack, designed the architecture, hired and mentored the team. That compressed learning is worth more than 7 years in a mature organization where someone else made the architecture decisions. And with my Databricks certification plus the MSc in AI, I bring a combination that's hard to find."

"You're right — my cloud experience is primarily AWS. But Databricks is cloud-agnostic by design. Unity Catalog, Delta Lake, PySpark — they work the same on Azure as on AWS. The Azure-specific pieces — storage accounts, ADLS Gen2, Azure DevOps — are the infrastructure layer, and I've already started studying for the DP-203 certification. The hard part is Databricks architecture, and that I already know."
