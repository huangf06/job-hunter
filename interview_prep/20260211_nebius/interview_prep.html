KEY PRINCIPLE

PART 7: PRACTICAL TIPS & REMINDERS

SPEAKING STRATEGY
  1. Speak slowly. You are not in a race. Slow = confident.
  2. Use short sentences. One idea per sentence. Period. Next sentence.
  3. Pause between paragraphs. Let the interviewer process what you said.
  4. Use structure words. "First... Second... Third..." or "The situation was... My action was... The result was..."
  5. It's okay to be brief. A clear 30-second answer beats a rambling 3-minute one.
BODY LANGUAGE ON ZOOM
  • Look at the camera, not the screen (simulates eye contact).
  • Sit up straight. Lean slightly forward = engaged.
  • Nod when the interviewer speaks = "I'm listening."
  • Smile naturally, especially at the start and end.
  • Keep hands visible. Use small gestures when explaining.
THINGS TO AVOID
  • Don't badmouth previous employers or China.
  • Don't say "I don't know" — say "I haven't worked with that specifically, but I'm a fast learner and I've worked with similar tools like..."
  • Don't give one-word answers. Always explain briefly.
  • Don't interrupt. Wait for them to finish, then respond.
  • Don't discuss politics (Yandex/Russia situation). Keep it factual and positive.
CLOSING STRONG
MEMORIZE THIS - End of interview

Thank you so much for your time today, Milly. I really enjoyed learning more about Nebius and the Data Engineer role. I am very excited about this opportunity, and I believe my experience with data pipelines, Spark, and Airflow would be a great fit for your team. I look forward to the next steps.
